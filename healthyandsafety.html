Best Hospitals in Vietnam

There are numerous state-owned and private hospitals in major cities like Hanoi, Danang, and Ho Chi Minh City that employ English-speaking staff. In rural areas, you may be far from the closest pharmacy or hospital, despite the fact that small pharmacies are common in almost every Vietnamese city.

Vietnam's public hospitals will not meet your expectations if you are traveling from Western Europe or North America. Additionally, it is likely that only Vietnamese will be spoken by the doctors, aides, and nurses. Public hospitals in Vietnam are likely to be overcrowded and inadequate for Western visitors.

On the other hand, a lot of private hospitals cater specifically to visitors and Western expats. Private hospitals in the major cities employ international-trained Vietnamese doctors in addition to those from Korea, France, Japan, and the United States. Those who speak English or French are much less likely to encounter difficulties with language barriers.


City International Hospital in Ho Chi Minh City is the country's top international hospital. It has 21 specialties, including general surgery, orthopedics, cardiology intervention, and neurology intervention, and it has 320 beds. Through its outpatient system, it serves over 400,000 patients annually, with 25% being foreigners.
