Culture of Vietnam in

Vietnam's Lunar New Year, Tet Nguyen Dan, or Tet for short, is the country's most significant annual celebration and public holiday that lasts from five to seven days.
Tet marks both the arrival of spring and the beginning of a new year. Traditional food, music, and dragon dancing on the streets are all part of the exuberant celebrations. This is a festive time to visit Vietnam because there is a real sense of celebration in the air.
Tet is a time of renewal that Vietnamese people use to honor their ancestors and get together with their families. People typically begin their preparations a week before Tet, when they begin to return home, clean their family graves, worship at their family altars, and plant kumquat trees, apricot blossoms, and peach blossoms, in their homes as a symbol of prosperity and well-being.
