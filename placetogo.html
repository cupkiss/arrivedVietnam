Travel in Vietnam

Vietnam has long been Southeast Asia's most popular destination. It's easy to see why: great food, gorgeous natural settings, and generally positive vibes. Prepare for an exciting journey into Vietnam's history, culture, and spirit of adventure with its numerous activities.

Despite appearances, this small nation is politically and culturally significant. While learning as much as you can about the Vietnam War, you can learn about the remarkable history of this resilient nation. The Gia Long Palace, which was once the South Vietnamese government's headquarters, is where the Ho Chi Minh City Museum is located. Explore the artifacts, photographs, and wartime relics that tell the story of this bloody era.

Take a train north toward Hoi An after you've explored the south. With its charming low-density urban design and traditional architecture, this stunning city is listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. You might be able to attend the Hoi An Lantern Festival, a monthly event in which thousands of paper lanterns are thrown into the night sky to celebrate the full moon, if your timing is right.

Take a ride on a cargo boat and travel north along the Mekong River, hoping to arrive near Hanoi. After you've finished your bowl of hot pho and looked around the city, head east toward Halong Bay. Its limestone islands and emerald-green waters will take your breath away. Explore the islands by kayak or snorkel, then use a junk boat to get back to the mainland.
