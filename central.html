The Central Of Vietnam

Take a stroll through the narrow streets of Hoi An's Ancient Town, where colorful lanterns illuminate the streets and timber frame shophouses still appear as they did centuries ago. Or you can learn about the last days of the Nguyen Dynasty in Hue, where the tranquil Perfume River is lined with royal citadels, tombs, and temples.

Danang City, a modern city in the middle, has one of the country's best beaches and some of Vietnam's most opulent resorts and golf courses. In addition, the stunning caves of Phong Nha, which are just a few hours away, are the most recent frontier for nature enthusiasts.

Top Three Destinations

Hoi An

In Hoi An, the bold march of progress slows down to a leisurely stroll. In this one-time exchanging port, the Thu Bon Stream wanders past disintegrating shop houses and endured pagodas, while sampans go back and forth from the old ship quay. Two-lane roads wind their way through rice fields and out of the Old Town to a frothy coastline. If you stay for a few days, Hoi An's mellow beauty and glimmering nightlife may completely seduce you. Things to do:Get lost in the Old Town, cycle in the countryside, hit the beach, marvel at My Son (a UNESCO Cultural Heritage Site 40 Kilometer)

Da Nang

It should come as no surprise that the people who live in Da Nang have some of the biggest smiles in the country. They have wide beaches, great street food, the famous Hai Van Pass, and a growing number of cafes, restaurants, and bars. The warm sands of My Khe Ocean side scope south from the rugged Child Tra Promontory. Although Da Nang is home to some of the most prestigious luxury resorts in Vietnam, the majority of the beach is still used by locals as a playground, gym, and source of income. (Click the image below to see 360 degree tour.)

Things to do:Enjoy the beach, see the Son TRa Peninsula, learn about Cham culture, dine on street food, explore Marble Mountains.

Hue

Hue is a city filled with stories. The Lords of the Nguyen Tradition assembled their primitive capital along Tint's prolific riverbanks and on its forested slopes, yet their magnificent inheritance is only one of many motivations to visit. Hue's leafy streets are lined with mossy pagodas, art deco mansions, and eye-popping markets, and its refined cuisine is legendary. The Perfume River flows through the entire scene at a leisurely pace that the rest of the city is happy to follow. (Click the image below to see 360 degree tour.)

Things to do:See Imperial Vietnam, eat like a king, explore the city's Buddhist roots, cycle in the rice paddies, revisit colonial history.
