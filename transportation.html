Motorcycles in Vietnam

Vietnam's traffic can be extremely chaotic. There are a lot of motorcycles all over, and it sometimes looks like there are no rules for driving.Surprisingly, it frequently appears to go very well. Driving in Vietnam as a foreigner is an adventure. Since motorbikes are used by Vietnamese people to get around, there are a lot of them everywhere. Yes, they occasionally drive in the wrong direction, cross pedestrian streets, and sit with four or more people! people riding a single motorcycle or transporting numerous items. In Vietnam, nothing is crazy at all!

Many roads and trail tracks were destroyed between 1955 and 1975 during the Vietnam War. Even though it took place decades ago, you can sometimes still see this. Although the roads in the more remote areas are generally worse, the main roads are generally good. Therefore, anticipate bumpy rides. Vietnam offers inexpensive modes of transportation, particularly buses, and trains. Although booking tickets in advance is not always required, it is recommended for trips and trains during holidays like the Vietnamese New Year. You shouldn't need to do much planning ahead if you don't travel during the holiday season in Vietnam.


Train in Vietnam

There are many other ways to get around Vietnam, so you don't have to drive a motorcycle or moped (though you could if you're an experienced driver) by bus, train, or plane, for instance.

Giving a opportunity to travel during the day to take in the stunning scenery from the train's window. There are rice fields, mountain ranges, small Vietnamese villages, buffalo, and a lot more. Things you won't get to see if you fly! There are sleeper trains in Vietnam with bunk beds, which are convenient if you don’t have much time to travel, or if you want to save money, as it saves you a night in a hotel. Although there is a network that runs from the south to the north, Vietnam does not have a particularly extensive train network. You could combine bus and train travel to get to some places.
