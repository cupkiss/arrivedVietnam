The South Of Vietnam

Begin your journey in Ho Chi Minh City, a bustling metropolis where modest mom-and-pop shops compete with cutting-edge skyscrapers for street space. The tranquil Mekong Delta is located beyond the city limits. Here, life takes place on the water and farmland extends as far as the eye can see.

Mui Ne, not far away, is known as Vietnam's kite-surfing capital and attracts vacationers with its laid-back beach vibe. Offshore, you'll find pristine coastlines and unspoiled landscapes on islands like Phu Quoc and Con Dao.

Top Three Destinations

Con Dao Island

Con Dao is a small archipelago off the southern coast that only a few tourists visit. Those who are willing to put in the extra effort are rewarded with some of the most blissful beaches and the best snorkeling in the country. The Con Dao National Park covers a significant portion of the island. You will have ample opportunities to discover your very own paradise within its dense jungle.

Things to do:Go beach-hunting, snorkeling and diving excursions, visit Con Dao's museum, cycle near the beach, relax in resort.

Phu Quoc Island

The pearl-like white sand beaches of Phu Quoc have given it the moniker "Pearl Island," but the island's efforts to protect the environment and its cultural heritage also merit attention. Since 2006, more than half of the laid-back island has been designated as a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve, and agriculture and fishing continue to be the primary industries. Phu Quoc, which is home to pearls, fish sauce, and pepper, offers both luxury and local life, with clear skies at night and clean water during the day.

Things to do:Enjoy water sports, short walks through the evergreen forest reward explorers with waterfalls, rock pools, and caves, discover VinWonders Phu Quoc - the largest theme park in Vietnam

Ho Chi Minh city

Ho Chi Minh City, formerly Saigon, is the economic center of Vietnam and the main hub for the southern region. It vibrates with innovation, energy, and a lot of traffic. The dynamic cityscape of HCMC, a bustling, cosmopolitan metropolis, brings together old and new Vietnam in the smallest of spaces, representing the city's past as well as its future.
