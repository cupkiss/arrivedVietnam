National Park in Vietnam

There is a great deal of variety in Vietnam's nature reserves and national parks. Rice terraces that are staggered and change with the seasons are abundant in the north. The center has the loudest waterfalls in the country and the largest caves in the world. Additionally, exotic wildlife and mangrove forests can be found in the south. The seven best national parks and nature reserves in Vietnam are listed below.

Phong Nha-Ke Bang National Park

Phong Nha-Ke Bang is a paradise for adventurers with tenacious rivers, vibrant jungles, and enormous caves. Many other systems are hidden beneath towering, jungle-topped karsts in this UNESCO-recognized national park, which is home to some of the largest caves in the world. Experiences in caving can last as little as an afternoon or as long as four days, depending on your level of interest. The national park offers kayaking, cycling, and hiking opportunities above these subterranean cave kingdoms. At the end of each day, you can return to the tranquil Phong Nha Town, which is a beautiful base.

Bach Ma National Park

Bach Ma is located halfway between Hue, the imperial city, and Hoi An, the ancient city. It is rarely visited. Since the early 20th century, the park has not seen much development, and the area still has a few French colonial villas that have been turned into charming guesthouses. There are sufficient hiking trails to keep you occupied for several days. Some go through waterfalls and rock pools. Walk up to the peak in the morning for a stunning view of the coast.

Cuc Phuong National Park

Cuc Phuong is Vietnam's most seasoned public park, blessed by Ho Chi Minh himself. It is home to two animal sanctuaries, a dense forest of trees that have been around for millennia, prehistoric caves, botanical gardens, and other interesting attractions. Illegally captured primate species are rescued and rehabilitated at the Endangered Primate Rescue Centre; while programs for breeding, conservation, and rescue are run by the Turtle Conservation Centre. Kayaking, bird watching, and trekking are some of your options; or reserve a stay in one of the friendly Muong people's stilt houses. The entrance fee to the park is 50.000 VND (roughtly $3).

Cat Ba National Park

Cat Ba, a large island in the Gulf of Tonkin, attracts more nature lovers each year with its captivating landscapes and beaches. Take in the breathtaking views from the tops of the mountains or test your legs on the network of trails that meander through the national park. Some of the more difficult hikes take you over mountain passes to Viet Hai, a remote, off-the-beaten-path village. There are a few basic homestays in Viet Hai if you want peace and quiet. One of the world's most endangered primates, the White-headed Langur, is a curious mammal to look out for.

Cat Tien National Park

Cat Tien has a plethora of activities to choose from. Without getting bored, you could easily stay here for a week or more. Those who enjoy hot jungle treks will enjoy Cat Then. You can see endemic wildlife like bears, crocodiles, bats, snakes, and tropical birds on multi-day tours deep into the parks. Take advantage of the opportunity to go on a night safari to meet fascinating insects and vibrant reptiles. You can kayak and boat tours through the park or cycle or motorbike through it on a network of roads if you prefer to stay off the ground. The tickets includes the round trip 2 minutes boat ride across the Dong Nai River.
